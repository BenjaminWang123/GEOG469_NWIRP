Safety & Support

Know Your Rights. Stay Calm. Ask for Help.

This page provides simple, community-centered guidance for people who may encounter immigration enforcement activity. It also connects people to support from Northwest Immigrant Rights Project.

Important Reminder

This page is educational and does not replace legal advice. If you or someone you know may be affected, contact a qualified legal organization as soon as possible.

Quick Guide

If You Encounter ICE Activity

These steps are designed to help people stay safer, avoid panic, and document what happened at a general community level.

1

Stay Calm

Do not run, argue, or physically interfere. Move to a safe place if you can.

2

Ask for Identification

If safe, observe whether officers identify themselves and whether they show a warrant.

3

Do Not Open the Door

You generally do not need to open your door unless officers show a valid judicial warrant.

4

Use Your Right to Silence

You can say: “I choose to remain silent. I want to speak with a lawyer.”

5

Document Safely

If it is safe, write down the county, time, agency markings, vehicles, and what happened.

6

Report the Impact

Submit a county-level report so advocates can better understand community impacts.

You Have Rights

Right to remain silent

You do not have to answer questions about where you were born or your immigration status.

Right to a lawyer

You can ask to speak with an attorney before answering questions or signing documents.

Right to refuse consent

You can refuse permission for a search if officers do not have proper legal authority.

Right to protect your family plan

Prepare emergency contacts, school pickup plans, and important documents in advance.

What To Report

Useful Information for Community Reporting

Reports should focus on community-level impacts. Do not include exact home addresses, private identifying details, or information that may expose someone to risk.

County or general area
Date and approximate time
Impact category: economic, health, education, or social stability
General description of what happened
Whether people missed work, school, healthcare, or public services
Optional image only if it is safe and does not expose people

Ask for Help

Contact Northwest Immigrant Rights Project

If you need legal support, community resources, or guidance after an immigration enforcement-related incident, contact NWIRP or a trusted legal service provider.

Before You Call

  • Prepare the county where the event happened.
  • Write down the approximate date and time.
  • Describe what happened in simple terms.
  • Do not share more personal details than necessary on public platforms.

Help Document Community Impacts

Your anonymized county-level report can help identify patterns affecting work, health, education, and daily life.
